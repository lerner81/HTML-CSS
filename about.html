Mark Lerner is a premier trombonist in the Columbus area. His versitile allows him to play in a large variety of bands. Mark plays everything from Classical to Funk. You may see him in at the Church of Messiah on Sunday mornings or out at the Park Street Tavern.

Mark was born into a musical family. He has three sisters and each one of them are outstanding musicians in their own right. His oldest sister, Michelle is an outstanding french horn player that went to OSU as a music major. Danette Lerner plays the saxophone and his youngest sister plays the trumpet.

Mark went to the Indiana School of Music for 2 years and studied Classical Trombone. At Indiana University, he studied with international trombonists Carle Lenthe and Jiggs Whigham. He then transfered to the Cincinnati-Conservatory of Music and finished his studies with a Bachelor's in Jazz Trombone. At the Cincinnati-Conservatory, he studied with local legend Jazz Trombonist Marc Fields.

After his college years, Mark went on to tour with Carnival Cruise Lines. Here, Mark played trombone in the main show lounge. He was quickly promoted to Musical Director. As Music Director, Mark collaborated with other bands on the ship and made special events. Everything from New years celebrations, 4th of July parties, and Mardi Gra parades.

Mark decided to settle down in his home-town of Columbus, Ohio. This is where you will find him continuing the art of music.
